F.A.Q

  • What Is MUN?

    Model United Nations (MUN) is an academic simulation of the United Nations where students play the role of delegates representing different countries and simulate UN committees. It aims to provide participants with a deeper understanding of international relations, diplomacy, and the UN's decision-making process.

  • How can I participate in UCIAMUN?

    To participate in UCIAMUN, you can register as a delegate through the official UCIAMUN website or contact the organizing committee for more information. Participants can also apply to serve as chairs or crisis staff for committees.

  • What can I expect from attending UCIAMUN?

    Attending UCIAMUN offers participants the opportunity to engage in substantive debates, negotiate resolutions, and interact with peers from different backgrounds. Additionally, participants can expect to enhance their public speaking, research, and critical thinking skills.

  • Are there any fees associated with participating in UCIAMUN?

    Details regarding registration fees and any associated costs will be provided in the registration forms available on the official UCIAMUN website.

  • How is UCIAMUN structured?

    UCIAMUN typically consists of multiple committees simulating different UN bodies, such as the General Assembly, Security Council, and specialized agencies. Each committee focuses on specific topics relevant to its mandate.
